# GigHunter AI launch kit

## Positioning

**One line:** A local-first AI freelancing copilot that finds public gigs, scores fit, filters scams, drafts proposals, and never auto-applies.

**Hook:** Stop hunting gigs. Make the gigs hunt you.

**Contrarian angle:** Most job agents optimize application volume. GigHunter automates research, not judgment.

## Hacker News

**Title:** Show HN: GigHunter AI, a local-first freelancing agent that refuses to auto-apply

**Post:**

I built GigHunter because finding freelance work meant living across job boards, while most automation tools tried to solve that by submitting generic applications at scale.

GigHunter takes the opposite approach. It reads public feeds and official APIs, deduplicates reposts, scores each gig against your profile, parses the real budget, filters scam signals, drafts three proposal angles, and reminds you to follow up. It deliberately never logs into a marketplace or presses Apply.

It runs locally with Ollama, has a dashboard, CLI, REST API, adaptive scoring from won/rejected gigs, and deterministic tests. MIT licensed.

Repo: https://github.com/abdulbasit742/gighunter-ai

I’d especially value feedback on source adapters, ranking signals, and whether the human-in-the-loop constraint feels like a feature or limitation.

## LinkedIn

Most AI job tools optimize for more applications. I think that’s the wrong goal.

Today I’m open-sourcing GigHunter AI, a local-first freelancing copilot that automates opportunity research without automating your judgment.

It finds gigs from public feeds and official APIs, scores fit against your skills, parses hidden budgets, filters scam signals, drafts tailored proposal variants, tracks follow-ups, and learns from wins and rejections. You still review and submit every application yourself.
