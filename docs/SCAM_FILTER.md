# Scam & red-flag filter

The fastest way to lose money freelancing is to spend time (or pay fees) on a gig that was never real. GigHunter screens every gig before it reaches the top of your list.

## What it catches
- Upfront / registration / training fees
- "Pay before work" or deposit requests
- Sketchy payment methods (gift cards, Western Union, crypto-only)
- Pushes to contact off-platform (Telegram/WhatsApp "directly")
- Too-good-to-be-true earnings ("$500/day, no experience")
- Inflated pay for low-skill busywork
- Unpaid / exposure / equity-only
- Requests for personal or financial info
- High-pressure urgency
- Suspiciously thin descriptions

## How it acts
Each gig gets a **risk score (0-100)** and a verdict:
- `clean` — passes through normally
- `caution` — score docked 15, flagged so you go in eyes-open
- `avoid` — score capped at 25 and **no proposal is auto-drafted**

Risky gigs are never silently deleted, they're demoted and labeled with the exact reasons (e.g. `asks for an upfront fee`, `sketchy payment method`) so you stay in control.

## Why it matters
Legit clients don't ask you to pay to work, don't need gift cards, and don't rush you off-platform on day one. This filter keeps your time on real, payable work.
